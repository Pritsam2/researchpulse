Data-Centric AI
Shifting the focus from model architecture to data quality, quantity, and lifecycle management
Data-centric Artificial Intelligence: A SurveyFoundational
ACM Computing Surveys📅 2025 · Vol 57(5), pp 1–42
Daochen Zha, Zaid Pervaiz Bhat, Kwei-Herng Lai, Fan Yang, Zhimeng Jiang, Shaochen Zhong, Xia Hu · Rice University & Texas A&M
▼
Daochen Zha · Rice University, Houston TX Xia Hu · Rice University Zaid Pervaiz Bhat · Texas A&M Kwei-Herng Lai · Texas A&M Fan Yang · Texas A&M

📐 Methodology & Framework

  • Systematic survey of the entire data-centric AI landscape across all modalities
  • 3-pillar framework: (1) Training Data Development, (2) Inference Data Development, (3) Data Maintenance
  • Training sub-categories: data collection, labeling, preparation, reduction, augmentation, pipeline search
  • Inference data: prompt engineering, context window optimization, in-context learning
  • Maintenance: data versioning, lineage tracking, continuous quality monitoring

💡 Key Findings

  • Model architecture improvements yield diminishing returns — data quality investments now dominate performance gains
  • Active labeling and automated data pipelines replace manual model tuning in production
  • Inference data development (prompting, context) is severely under-researched
  • Data maintenance is the least formalized pillar — significant open problems remain
  • Paper has become the go-to citation for positioning data-centric research

🎓 PhD Research Angle

This is the foundational citation for any data-centric thesis — cite it in your introduction to frame the broader paradigm shift. The "pipeline search" subcategory (AutoML for data pipelines) and "inference data development" (how data is structured at test time) both point to underexplored thesis directions. If you work in label noise, synthetic data, or dataset quality, you will position your thesis within the framework this paper establishes. Daochen Zha and Xia Hu at Rice University are strong contacts to reach out to for PhD inquiries.

Label Noise Learning
Training robust models when ground-truth annotations are corrupted, crowdsourced, or web-scraped
A Survey on Learning from Data with Label Noise via Deep Neural Networks
Systems Science & Control Engineering (Taylor & Francis)📅 April 2025 · Vol 13(1)
Comprehensive taxonomy of noise types and DNN-based LLN approaches · Benchmarks: CIFAR-10N, Clothing1M, WebVision
▼

📐 Taxonomy of Methods

  • Noise types: symmetric, asymmetric, class-dependent (CDN), instance-dependent (IDN)
  • Approach 1 — Robust loss: Mean Absolute Error (MAE), symmetric cross-entropy, generalized cross-entropy
  • Approach 2 — Loss adjustment: Label smoothing, noise transition matrices, bootstrapping
  • Approach 3 — Sample selection: Small-loss trick, GMM-based selection (DivideMix), co-training
  • Approach 4 — Semi-supervised: MixMatch-style relabeling, MentorNet, curriculum learning
  • Benchmarks: CIFAR-10N (real annotator noise), Clothing1M (1M web images, 38.5% noise), WebVision

💡 Key Findings

  • IDN is the unsolved frontier — real-world noise depends on individual sample features, not just class
  • Sample selection (small-loss trick) remains most practical but suffers self-confirmation bias on high noise rates
  • Semi-supervised methods outperform pure supervised when noise exceeds 40%
  • NLP label noise is significantly less studied than vision — major gap
  • Multimodal label noise (image-text misalignment) is an emerging open problem

🎓 PhD Research Angle

Instance-dependent noise is the realistic, unsolved case. CDN assumes all samples in a class have the same flip probability — but in medical imaging, a blurry X-ray is harder to label correctly than a clear one. IDN captures this. A PhD thesis on IDN estimation (especially for medical, scientific, or low-resource settings) addresses a genuine unsolved problem. Standard benchmarks CIFAR-10N and Clothing1M are your validation testbeds. NLP label noise is particularly sparse — a thesis bridging label noise and NLP/LLMs could be very high-impact.

CLIPCleaner: Cleaning Noisy Labels with CLIPHigh Impact
ACM Multimedia 2024 (ACM MM'24)📅 2024 · ACM International Conference on Multimedia
Chen Feng, Georgios Tzimiropoulos, Ioannis Patras · University College London (UCL) & Queen Mary University of London
▼
Chen Feng · UCL, London Georgios Tzimiropoulos · UCL / Samsung AI Centre London Ioannis Patras · Queen Mary University of London

📐 Methodology

  • Uses CLIP (OpenAI's vision-language model) as an offline zero-shot clean sample selector
  • Zero-shot classifier: CLIP image embeddings aligned against text descriptions of each class
  • Applied before training — eliminates "self-confirmation bias" of in-training confidence methods
  • Handles "hard noise" (mislabeling between visually similar classes) via rich semantic CLIP representations
  • Plug-in compatible: works as pre-filter for DivideMix, SOP, and other LLN frameworks

💡 Key Findings

  • Outperforms small-loss and GMM-based selection on CIFAR-10N and Clothing1M
  • Especially strong on hard noise — CLIP semantic space separates similar-looking classes better than pixel features
  • Zero-shot approach removes dependency on in-training model quality entirely
  • Efficient: CLIP inference is offline and fast vs. training-time methods
  • Published at ACM MM — top venue for multimedia + ML cross-over research

🎓 PhD Research Angle

This paper demonstrates a key 2025 trend: foundation models repurposed as data quality tools. Extensions to explore: (1) applying CLIP-style zero-shot selection to NLP noisy labels using text embedding models, (2) combining CLIPCleaner with active learning to minimally query humans for ambiguous samples, (3) extending to instance-dependent noise detection where noise correlates with CLIP uncertainty scores. The "offline pre-filter" idea also applies to large-scale dataset curation — highly relevant for LLM pretraining data quality.

Data Quality in Machine Learning
Measuring, monitoring, and correcting data quality across the full ML pipeline — from acquisition to production
End-to-End Data Quality-Driven Framework for Machine Learning in ProductionDec 2025
arXiv Preprint📅 December 2025 · arXiv:2512.19723
Firas Bayram, Bestoun S. Ahmed, Erik Hallin
▼
Firas Bayram Bestoun S. Ahmed Erik Hallin

📐 Methodology

  • 3-component framework: (1) dynamic drift detection, (2) adaptive data quality metrics, (3) MLOps feedback loop
  • Real-time quality signals integrated into deployed ML model decision-making
  • Adaptive thresholds: quality gates adjust dynamically based on live data statistics
  • Designed for minimal overhead — sub-millisecond quality checks in the serving path
  • Validated on production-scale streaming datasets

💡 Key Findings

  • Quality-driven decision gates reduce model degradation by catching drift before accuracy drops
  • Adaptive metrics outperform static thresholds significantly on non-stationary data streams
  • LLMs are emerging as data quality auditors — validated for text data quality checking
  • Bridges gap between offline data quality research and online ML deployment reality

🎓 PhD Research Angle

MLOps + data quality is a severely underexplored intersection. Most academic data quality research assumes static, offline datasets — but production models see shifting data every day. Research extensions: (1) federated learning quality monitoring (data quality across heterogeneous clients), (2) LLM fine-tuning data quality signals (can we detect quality issues before they harm fine-tuned models?), (3) causal approaches to identifying which quality dimensions caused a specific model failure. All are publishable at top ML/data venues (VLDB, SIGMOD, NeurIPS).

The Effects of Data Quality on Machine Learning Performance on Tabular Data
Information Systems (Elsevier)📅 2025
Controlled study: 7 data quality dimensions × 10 ML algorithms on real tabular benchmark datasets
▼

📐 Methodology

  • Controlled injection of 7 quality issues: missing values, label noise, outliers, inconsistencies, duplicates, class imbalance, feature irrelevance
  • 10 ML algorithms: Logistic Regression, SVM, kNN, Decision Tree, Random Forest, XGBoost, and neural baselines
  • Severity levels varied systematically (0%, 10%, 20%, 40% contamination)
  • Evaluated across multiple real-world tabular benchmark datasets (UCI + Kaggle)

💡 Key Findings

  • Most damaging: label noise and missing values — affect all algorithms at all severity levels
  • Tree-based methods (Random Forest, XGBoost) most robust to mild quality issues
  • Linear models (LR, SVM) most sensitive to outliers and feature irrelevance
  • No algorithm dominates across all quality scenarios — model selection should be quality-aware
  • Duplicates have minimal impact until extreme rates (>30%) — counterintuitive finding

🎓 PhD Research Angle

This paper provides empirical grounding to motivate any data quality correction method. Use it as a baseline: "given that label noise at 20% degrades accuracy by X%, our method recovers Y%." The tabular domain is underserved vs. image/text — a thesis focused on tabular data quality (common in finance, healthcare, insurance) can make a strong contribution. The finding that model selection should be quality-aware opens a direction connecting data quality to AutoML.

Synthetic Data Generation
AI-generated training data — scaling promises, model collapse pitfalls, and prevention strategies
AI Models Collapse When Trained on Recursively Generated DataLandmark · Nature
Nature (2024) + ICLR 2025 Extensions📅 2024–2025
Ilia Shumailov et al. · University of Oxford & Google DeepMind
▼
Ilia Shumailov · Oxford / Google DeepMind Oxford Computer Science Google DeepMind Team

📐 Methodology

  • Empirical + theoretical analysis of iterative training on model-generated synthetic outputs
  • Three compounding error sources: statistical approximation, functional expressivity, functional approximation
  • Experiments across: GPT-family language models, GANs (vision), diffusion models (images)
  • Tracks tail-distribution coverage across training generations (Gen 0 → Gen 5+)
  • ICLR 2025 "Strong Model Collapse" paper extends with formal theoretical bounds

💡 Key Findings

  • Recursive training on unfiltered synthetic data causes irreversible distribution shift
  • Rare phenomena forgotten by generation 3–4 — catastrophic for medical AI, safety systems, and edge-case coverage
  • Prevention: (1) maintain real data fraction, (2) active curation of synthetic data (gap-filling), (3) entropy/MMD-based quality filtering
  • Watermarking + provenance tracking enables safe large-scale data curation

🎓 PhD Research Angle

Model collapse detection is a wide-open frontier. The original paper identifies the problem; reliable automatic detection metrics don't yet exist. Publishable PhD directions: (1) early-warning metrics for collapse in continuous training pipelines, (2) domain-specific collapse in medical/scientific AI where rare events are most critical, (3) optimal real/synthetic mixing ratios under distribution shift. This also connects to dataset quality assessment — collapsed models produce lower-quality synthetic data, creating a measurable feedback loop you can study. Published in Nature — very high citation impact for your literature review.

Nature Paper → ICLR 2025 Extension
How to Synthesize Text Data without Model Collapse?ICML 2025
ICML 2025📅 2025 · Vancouver, BC
Prevention-focused companion to the Shumailov collapse paper — actionable guidelines for safe synthetic data pipelines
▼

📐 Methodology

  • Systematic comparison of synthetic data strategies: random sampling vs. active curation vs. gap-filling
  • Real data anchoring: maintain a fixed fraction of human-generated data across generations
  • Quality metrics: Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) and entropy to ensure synthetic data adds new information
  • Diversity preservation: reject synthetic outputs that near-duplicate real data
  • Text-focused evaluation on generation and reasoning tasks

💡 Key Findings

  • Regular real data injection prevents collapse even in multi-generation pipelines
  • Entropy-based filtering maintains tail-distribution coverage — preserves rare phenomena
  • Active curation (gap-filling) outperforms random synthetic sampling significantly
  • Watermarking enables safe provenance tracking at scale
  • Best practice: synthetic data fills distribution gaps, real data anchors rare events

🎓 PhD Research Angle

Together with the Shumailov paper, this completes a problem-solution arc. A PhD could specialize in domain-specific synthetic data pipelines — for scientific literature (where rare findings matter most), clinical notes, or low-resource languages where human data is scarce. The gap-filling idea also connects to active learning: which samples should you generate synthetically to maximally improve model coverage?

Trustworthy AI
Hallucination, alignment, explainability, and robustness converging into one unified research challenge
Mapping Trustworthiness in Large Language Models: A Bibliometric Analysis
arXiv📅 March 2025 · arXiv:2503.04785
José Siqueira de Cerqueira, Kai-Kristian Kemell, Rebekah Rousi, Nannan Xi, Juho Hamari, Pekka Abrahamsson · Tampere University, Finland
▼
José Siqueira de Cerqueira · Tampere University, Finland Kai-Kristian Kemell · Tampere University Pekka Abrahamsson · Tampere University Nannan Xi · Tampere University Juho Hamari · Tampere University

📐 Methodology

  • Bibliometric mapping of 2,006 publications from Web of Science (2019–Feb 2025)
  • Bibliometrix R-package analysis + citation network mapping
  • 7,901 researchers analyzed; avg. 8.9 citations/document; 96.6% multi-author studies
  • Manual deep review of 68 most-cited papers for qualitative synthesis
  • Keyword co-occurrence clusters: transparency, alignment, hallucination, robustness, explainability

💡 Key Findings

  • Field exploded: 3 papers (2019) → 1,434 papers (2024) — 478× growth in 5 years
  • 18 competing definitions of "trustworthiness" — no consensus in the field
  • Top 3 dimensions studied: transparency, explainability, reliability
  • Top 2 enhancement strategies: fine-tuning (dominant) and RAG (rapidly growing)
  • Hallucination, alignment, and adversarial robustness now treated as one unified problem

🎓 PhD Research Angle

The 18 competing definitions signal that the field lacks a unifying framework — a gap a PhD thesis could address. With 478× growth, this is a high-competition, high-impact field. Less saturated directions: (1) trustworthiness in agentic/multi-agent AI systems, (2) cross-cultural and cross-lingual trust dimensions (Western-centric research dominated), (3) trustworthiness under distribution shift and open-world conditions. Connecting trustworthiness to data quality (pretraining data determines intrinsic hallucination) is a novel cross-topic angle that few papers address systematically.

Large Language Models Hallucination: A Comprehensive SurveyOct 2025
arXiv📅 October 2025 · arXiv:2510.06265
Full taxonomy of LLM hallucination types across the development lifecycle with mitigation evaluation
▼

📐 Methodology

  • Novel hallucination taxonomy: intrinsic (factual errors), extrinsic (unverifiable claims), reasoning errors
  • Analysis across full LLM lifecycle: pretraining data → fine-tuning → inference → deployment
  • Empirical evaluation of mitigation strategies: CoT prompting, RAG, RLHF, fine-tuning, hybrid
  • Framework for attributing hallucination to prompting strategy vs. model behavior

💡 Key Findings

  • Pretraining data quality is the root cause of intrinsic hallucination — directly connecting trustworthiness to data quality
  • RAG significantly reduces factual hallucination but introduces retrieval-quality dependency
  • CoT prompting reduces hallucination 15–30% vs. direct answering — prompt engineering matters
  • Hallucination rates compound in agentic chains — amplifies errors
  • No single mitigation dominates; hybrid approaches (RAG + CoT + fine-tuning) work best

🎓 PhD Research Angle

The finding that pretraining data quality drives intrinsic hallucination creates a powerful bridge between Trustworthy AI and Data-Centric AI. A thesis framed as "data-centric med as "data-centric approaches to reducing LLM hallucination" is novel, timely, and spans both communities. Specific directions: (1) dataset quality metrics that predict downstream hallucination rate, (2) data curation strategies that specifically target hallucination-inducing training examples, (3) hallucination in agentic systems where errors compound.

Dataset Quality Assessment
Principled, scalable methods for scoring and evaluating the contribution and quality of training datasets
Chunked Data Shapley: A Scalable Dataset Quality Assessment for Machine LearningCIKM 2025
ACM CIKM 2025📅 November 2025 · Seoul, Republic of Korea
Andreas Loizou, Dimitrios Tsoumakos
▼
Andreas Loizou Dimitrios Tsoumakos

📐 Methodology

  • Core idea: group data tuples into chunks, compute Shapley value per chunk instead of per individual datapoint
  • Standard Data Shapley: exponential in dataset size (NP-hard) — C-DaSh makes it polynomial
  • Chunk granularity is a tunable hyperparameter: coarser chunks = faster but less precise
  • Supports both classification and regression pipelines out of the box
  • Evaluated on large tabular datasets where standard Shapley is computationally infeasible

💡 Key Findings

  • 80×–2300× speedup over standard Data Shapley methods
  • Accurately identifies low-quality data regions on real benchmark datasets
  • Outperforms existing Shapley approximations in both efficiency and detection accuracy
  • Practical at scale: first Shapley-based method usable on industry-size tabular datasets
  • Published at CIKM 2025 — top-tier ACM venue for information and knowledge management

🎓 PhD Research Angle

Scalable dataset valuation is one of the biggest open problems in data-centric AI. C-DaSh closes the computational gap but chunk-level attribution loses individual-sample resolution. Extensions: (1) adaptive chunk sizing based on local data density — smaller chunks in heterogeneous regions, (2) applying to LLM pretraining corpora (trillions of tokens — Shapley at any granularity is unsolved at this scale), (3) combining with active learning (acquire high-Shapley-value data first), (4) data valuation for data marketplaces and licensing — a growing commercial area with open research problems. Published at CIKM — your target venue if you work on this topic.

The METRIC-Framework for Assessing Data Quality for Trustworthy AI in Medicine
npj Digital Medicine (Nature Portfolio)📅 2024 · Vol 7, Article 203
D. Schwabe, K. Becker, M. Seyferth, A. Klaß, T. Schaeffter · Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin, Germany
▼
D. Schwabe · Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin T. Schaeffter · Charité Berlin / PTB K. Becker M. Seyferth A. Klaß

📐 Methodology

  • PRISMA systematic review: 5,408 studies screened → 120 eligible papers analyzed in depth
  • Synthesizes literature into 15 data quality dimensions for medical ML datasets
  • Dimensions cover: completeness, consistency, accuracy, timeliness, representativeness, provenance, annotation quality, privacy compliance, and 7 more
  • Checklist format validated across imaging (CT/MRI), EHR, and genomics datasets
  • Developed in context of EU AI Act and FDA regulatory requirements for medical AI

💡 Key Findings

  • 15 dimensions are necessary; current practice addresses only 4–6 on average
  • Annotation quality and representativeness are the most-neglected in practice
  • Regulatory approval of medical AI is increasingly gated on data quality documentation
  • Framework adopted outside medicine as a general-purpose dataset QA template
  • Published in Nature Portfolio — high citation impact, strong for your bibliography

🎓 PhD Research Angle

The 15-dimension framework is gaining adoption outside medicine — which signals an opportunity to generalize and validate it across other regulated high-stakes domains: legal AI, financial AI, autonomous systems, and scientific AI. A PhD in medical informatics, health AI, or AI safety could build automated tools for each of the 15 dimensions (currently manual). The connection to EU AI Act compliance is also significant — regulatory-grade data quality assessment is a growing applied research area with both academic and industry funding opportunities. Charité Berlin and T. Schaeffter are strong contacts if you are interested in medical AI.

Instance-Dependent Label Noise at Scale
Class-dependent noise is well-studied. IDN — where noise depends on individual sample features — is the realistic unsolved case. Medical imaging, scientific datasets, and LLM annotation are natural application domains. Testbeds: CIFAR-10N, Clothing1M.

Scalable Dataset Valuation for LLM Corpora
C-DaSh handles tabular ML. Principled Shapley-based valuation of trillion-token pretraining corpora is unsolved — and commercially critical for data licensing, provenance tracking, and responsible AI. Connects to data marketplaces.
📊 Dataset Quality

Automatic Model Collapse Detection
Shumailov et al. identified the problem; no reliable auto-detection metric exists yet. An early-warning system for collapse in continuous training pipelines is a concrete, publishable contribution — relevant to every lab training on synthetic data.
🧬 Synthetic Data
04
Unified Trustworthiness Framework
18 competing definitions with no consensus (per the bibliometric study). A formal, measurable framework unifying hallucination, alignment, explainability, and robustness is a major open problem — and would be a highly-cited foundational contribution.
🛡️ Trustworthy AI
05
Online Data Quality for Streaming ML
Most research assumes static datasets. Joint drift + quality monitoring for continuously-deployed models is early-stage. Extensions to federated learning (quality heterogeneity across clients) and LLM serving pipelines are largely open.
✅ Data Quality in ML

Foundation Models as Data Auditors
CLIPCleaner hints at this for vision. Using LLMs/VLMs to audit, clean, and curate training data across modalities — text, tabular, multimodal — has no dominant approach in 2026. Connects data-centric AI to foundation model research.
